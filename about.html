About

The Geneseo Digital Humanities Research Group studies how digital tools and quantitative methods can improve research in the Humanities, cultural studies, and social sciences. In using these cutting edge methodologies, our group augments the traditional research experiences in these fields, just as computational science did to the physical sciences in the 1950's and 1960's. As a group, we like to push for a global view when considering research questions, hoping that doing so will accelerate the rate at which our scholarly discovery can occur. Speeding up the discovery phase, ultimately reduces the amount of mundane work involved in research and allows us to spend more time on exceptional and complex problems. By iterating our process, we are able to combine our digital and quantitative methodologies with human intuition to first identify potentially interesting problems and then attempt to explain them within an appropriate context.
